COMPARING LEARNING PRINCIPLES

LEARNING PRINCIPLES 7 LEARNING PRINCIPLES (OECD) STUDENTS’ LEARNING EXPERIENCES IN SENIOR PRIMARY/SECONDARY NAMIBIA BASIC EDUCATION CURRICULUM (the direct words & page number)
1. Learners at the centre “In terms of teacher interaction, the best experiences we as learners had with teachers involved a combination of factors such as clear communication, approachability, enthusiasm for the subject, encouragement, personalized attention, constructive feedback, and creating a supportive environment”
2. The social nature of learning “Regarding fellow learner interaction, Working together with classmates who were courteous, helpful, and eager to impart knowledge improved our educational experience”
3. Emotions are integral to learning - “Some teachers displayed unprofessional behaviour by reacting angrily to minor issues and going as far as dismissing students from class, disrupting their learning experience”

- “bullying created a hostile environment that not only impacted the victim but also instilled fear and anxiety in us and other students, further impeding our learning process.”
4. Recognising individual differences “adapting teaching methods to cater to diverse learning styles and needs”
5. Stretching ALL learners “teachers inspired us by giving us a positive and engaging learning environment, and by adapting their teaching methods to cater to diverse learning styles and needs, we felt valued and motivated to excel. Overall, these best experiences with our teachers left a lasting impact on us, shaping our attitudes towards education and our potential for growth.”
6. Assessment for learning “Peer-to-peer learning activities, projects, and group discussions”
7. Building horizontal connections “Peer-to-peer learning activities, projects, and group discussions helped us improve our communication and cooperation abilities while also fostering a sense of community.”
